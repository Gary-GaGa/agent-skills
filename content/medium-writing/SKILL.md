# Medium Writing and Audience Growth

> Writing well is necessary; writing for the platform is what makes it travel. Medium rewards readability, structure, and engagement — design your articles for all three.

## When to Use This Skill

- Writing or polishing a Medium article
- Planning a Medium publishing strategy
- Improving formatting and reading experience
- Setting up SEO, tags, and exposure tactics
- Building a personal brand and reader community

---

## 1. Structure and Writing Framework

### 1.1 Title

- **Length:** 50–80 characters; concise and punchy
- **Tactics:** numbers, questions, "How to..." phrasing, or an explicit value promise
- **Examples:**
  - ✅ "5 Formatting Tricks That Make Medium Articles Travel"
  - ✅ "Why Nobody Reads Your Medium Posts — 3 Reasons"
  - ❌ "Some Thoughts About Writing" (too vague)
- Long titles get truncated on recommendation cards — front-load the keyword.

### 1.2 Subtitle

- One line that completes the value promise the title couldn't fit
- Add a keyword to support SEO
- Aim for 100–160 characters

### 1.3 Hook (opening)

- The first 2–3 sentences decide whether the reader continues
- Effective patterns:
  - State a pain point or shared frustration
  - Open with a striking statistic
  - Tell a brief personal story
  - Promise the reader exactly what they'll learn
- Avoid long preambles or self-introductions.

### 1.4 Body structure

- **One idea per paragraph.** Each paragraph carries a single concept.
- Use "intro → detail → recap" or "problem → analysis → solution"
- Use transitional sentences to maintain rhythm
- For longer pieces, use H2/H3 to create a built-in table of contents

### 1.5 Closing (CTA)

- Recap the central point in 1–2 sentences
- Add a Call to Action:
  - Invite claps
  - Ask for comments
  - Suggest follow / subscribe
  - Link to a related piece

## 2. Formatting (the Readability Lever)

### 2.1 Paragraphs

- **2–4 sentences (3–5 lines) per paragraph.** Avoid walls of text.
- Blank lines between paragraphs give the page room to breathe.

### 2.2 Heading hierarchy

- **H1** — the article title (only one)
- **H2** — major section
- **H3** — sub-section
- Don't skip levels (H2 directly to H4)
- Headings should be descriptive — avoid "Intro", "Conclusion".

### 2.3 Lists

- Step-by-step → ordered (numbered) list
- Parallel points → unordered (bullet) list
- Each item: 1–2 sentences, concise.

### 2.4 Emphasis and inline marks

- **Bold** — core keyword or key sentence (1–2 per paragraph maximum)
- *Italic* — for citations, terms of art, supplementary notes
- `Inline code` — technical terms or commands
- Block quote — pull quotes, key insights, attributed lines
- Don't over-emphasize; it dilutes meaning.

### 2.5 Horizontal rules

- Use Medium's separator to break large sections or topic shifts.
- Good places: between sections, viewpoint pivots, story-to-analysis transitions.

### 2.6 Images and visuals

- **Hero image:** mandatory, high quality, on-topic — drives the recommendation card
- Add captions and source attribution
- Interleave images every 300–500 words
- Avoid stacking multiple images consecutively
- Image alt text contributes to SEO; describe what's in the image and include a relevant keyword.

## 3. SEO

### 3.1 Title (Meta Title)

- Front-load the core keyword
- Keep within ~60 characters so Google search results don't truncate

### 3.2 Subtitle (Meta Description)

- Medium's subtitle becomes the meta description
- 100–160 characters; describe the article's value clearly
- Naturally include 1–2 keywords

### 3.3 Custom URL

- Edit the slug before publishing to short, keyword-rich English
- Example: `medium.com/@username/medium-writing-tips`
- Avoid the auto-generated hash slug.

### 3.4 In-body keyword placement

- Core keyword appears in: title, subtitle, first paragraph, H2 headings
- Use natural synonyms and related terms
- Don't keyword-stuff — readability comes first.

### 3.5 Image SEO

- Add alt text to every image
- Describe content and include a relevant keyword.

## 4. Tag Strategy

### 4.1 Basic rules

- Up to **5 tags** per article
- Recommended mix:
  - **Big tags** ×1–2: large following (e.g. `Writing`, `Self Improvement`)
  - **Niche tags** ×1–2: precise audience (e.g. `Newsletter`, `Personal Branding`)
  - **Industry tags** ×1: discipline-specific (e.g. `Software Engineering`, `Finance`)

### 4.2 Common tags by topic

| Topic | Tag examples |
|-------|--------------|
| Writing | `Writing`, `Self Publishing`, `Creator Economy` |
| Marketing | `Content Marketing`, `SEO`, `Audience Growth` |
| Personal growth | `Self Improvement`, `Productivity`, `Reading` |
| Technical | `Programming`, `Software Engineering`, `AI` |
| Platform | `Medium`, `Blogging`, `Publication` |

### 4.3 Tag optimization

- Look at the tags top articles in your niche use
- Prefer tags with established follower counts
- Periodically review; rotate tags based on data.

## 5. Platform Strategy

### 5.1 Cadence

- 1–2 articles per week is a sustainable target
- Quality > quantity
- Publishing on a consistent schedule trains readers.

### 5.2 Publications

- Submit to relevant publications to leverage their existing audience
- Consider the major publications in your niche
- You can also create your own publication to consolidate your work.

### 5.3 Engagement

- Reply to every comment to build relationships
- Read, clap, and comment on others' work
- Use Highlights to interact with passages.

### 5.4 Cross-platform amplification

- Share to Twitter / X, LinkedIn, Hacker News, Reddit (where appropriate)
- Use Friend Links so non-paying readers can read paywalled posts
- Run a newsletter to keep readers in your own list (see [`newsletter-writing`](../newsletter-writing/SKILL.md)).

### 5.5 Analytics

- Watch Medium Stats:
  - **Views:** impressions
  - **Reads:** completed reads
  - **Read Ratio:** completion rate (target ≥ 30%, excellent ≥ 50%)
  - **Fans:** clappers
- Low read ratio → check length, hook, formatting
- High views, low reads → title attractive but content didn't match.

## 6. Medium Partner Program (Earnings)

### 6.1 Eligibility

- Medium membership account
- At least one published article
- Stripe payout configured

### 6.2 How earnings are computed

- Primary signal: **time members spend reading your articles**
- Engagement (claps, comments, highlights) influences distribution
- Submitting to publications increases reach and read time.

### 6.3 Earning more

- Long-form depth (1,500–3,000 words) increases dwell time
- Read ratio matters more than raw views
- Series posts pull readers through multiple pieces.

## 7. Article Templates

### 7.1 Tutorial template

```
# [Number] [Topic] [Outcome / Tactic]

[1–2 sentence hook: pain point or curious question]

[1 sentence on what the reader will learn]

---

## 1. [First key point]

[Explanation + example or image]

## 2. [Second key point]

[Explanation + example or image]

## 3. [Third key point]

[Explanation + example or image]

---

## Wrap-up

[1–2 sentences of takeaway]

[CTA: invite engagement]
```

### 7.2 Experience-share template

```
# [Personal experience] — [What you learned / outcome]

[Open with a scene or story so the reader can picture it]

---

## Background

[Set the stage: situation, challenge, problem]

## What I did

[Concrete steps and decisions]

## What I learned

[Quantifiable result + reflection]

---

## My advice to you

[Actionable recommendations]

[CTA]
```

### 7.3 Opinion / commentary template

```
# [Your stance on a trend or phenomenon]

[Hook: news headline, data point, or observation]

---

## What's happening

[Objective description]

## What I'm seeing

[Personal observation + argument]

## Why it matters

[Implications, prediction]

---

## What you can do

[Reader actions]

[CTA]
```

## 8. Pre-Publish Checklist

- [ ] Title is concise, punchy, includes keyword
- [ ] Subtitle adds value beyond the title
- [ ] First 3 sentences hook attention
- [ ] One idea per paragraph, ≤ 5 lines
- [ ] H2/H3 hierarchy in place
- [ ] Lists or pull quotes break the text wall
- [ ] Bold / italic used judiciously
- [ ] Hero image is high quality and on-topic
- [ ] Images have captions and alt text
- [ ] Custom URL slug is short and keyword-relevant
- [ ] 5 well-chosen tags
- [ ] Closing has a clear CTA
- [ ] Mobile preview reads cleanly

## 9. Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Counter |
|---------|---------|
| Paragraphs > 7 lines | 2–4 sentences each |
| No subheadings | Insert H2/H3 every 200–400 words |
| Vague, low-pull title | Specific, numbered, verb-led |
| Tags too narrow | Mix big tags + niche tags |
| No hero image | Always include a quality hero |
| Skipping SEO setup | Custom slug + keyword in subtitle |
| No engagement after publishing | Reply, read others, build presence |
| Volume over value | Each post needs a clear value promise |
