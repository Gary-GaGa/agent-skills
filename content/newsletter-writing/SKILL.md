# Newsletter Writing and Growth

> A newsletter's core advantage is **owning the reader list**. Social platforms change algorithms; accounts get banned. The email list is yours.

## When to Use This Skill

- Starting a newsletter from zero
- Converting Medium / social readers into subscribers
- Improving open rate and click rate
- Designing a sustainable writing cadence
- Building a personal brand via newsletter

---

## 1. Platform Choice

| Platform | Best for | Free tier | Paid subscriptions |
|----------|----------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Substack** | Solo writers | Unlimited subscribers | ✅ 10% platform fee |
| **beehiiv** | Growth-driven, analytics | 2,500 subscribers | ✅ |
| **Buttondown** | Developers, minimalist | 100 subscribers | ✅ |
| **ConvertKit** | Advanced automation, sales | 1,000 subscribers | ✅ |
| **Revue (X)** | Discontinued | — | — |

1. **Start with Substack.** Zero setup, built-in SEO, social discovery.
2. **For more control and analytics, consider beehiiv.** Substack's analytics are limited.
3. **Don't burn time on platform choice up front.** What matters is starting to write — the tool is secondary.

---

## 2. Zero to One

### Launch checklist

- [ ] Topic positioning in one sentence: "I help ____ solve ____."
- [ ] A memorable newsletter name
- [ ] A 50-character pitch (why subscribe?)
- [ ] Send the first issue (it doesn't need to be perfect — it needs to exist)
- [ ] Put the subscribe link on every social profile, your Medium author page, and GitHub bio

### Initial outreach

4. **Start from your existing network.** Post on socials, DM 10 likely-interested friends, add the subscribe link at the bottom of every Medium piece.
5. **The first 100 subscribers are the hardest.** After that, word-of-mouth and search start helping.
6. **Don't buy lists.** Bought subscribers don't read, drag down your open rate, and risk getting flagged as spam.

## 3. Content Planning

### Content types

| Type | Example | Cadence |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Long-form deep dive** | Tutorials, analysis | 1–2 / month |
| **Curated digest** | "5 things worth reading this week" + commentary | Weekly |
| **Personal opinion** | Your take on a current event | Ad hoc |
| **Tool review** | A real review of a specific tool | Monthly |
| **Q&A / reader mailbag** | Answer reader questions | Monthly |

### Planning principles

7. **Cadence beats frequency.** Tuesday 8am sharp > "whenever I'm free".
8. **One issue, one theme.** Don't cram. Average open-to-read is < 3 minutes.
9. **80% value, 20% promotion.** A newsletter's value is the content, not the marketing.
10. **Build series.** "Stock-market weekly", "AI tools monthly" — series train reader expectation.

---

## 4. Writing Tactics

### Subject line

11. **≤ 50 characters.** Mobile preview only shows ~40 characters.
12. **Promise something specific.** ✅ "3 Go tips that make tests 5× faster" ❌ "This week's share"
13. **Numbers, questions, or direct benefits.** The reason to open should land in 2 seconds.

### Opening

14. **The first 2 lines decide whether the reader continues.** Lead with the point or a curious question.
15. **Don't open with "Hi everyone, this is [your name]".** They subscribed — they know.

### Layout

16. **Short paragraphs (2–3 sentences).** Email reading width is narrower than the web.
17. **Bold the key sentence in each paragraph.** Skim readers only see the bold.
18. **A visual change every ~200 words.** Subheadings, lists, blockquotes, separators.

### Closing

19. **One clear CTA.** Reply, share, click, vote — pick one.
20. **Don't pile up 5 CTAs.** Too many choices = no action.

## 5. Growth Strategies

| Strategy | Effect | Difficulty |
|----------|--------|------------|
| **Social cross-promo** | Mention the newsletter on Twitter/X, Threads, LinkedIn | Low |
| **Cross-promotion** | Mutually recommend with newsletters in adjacent niches | Medium |
| **Lead magnet** | Give a free resource (PDF, template) in exchange for subscription | Medium |
| **Guest issues** | Write a guest issue on another newsletter | Med-high |
| **SEO (Substack)** | Substack posts are indexed by Google | Low (automatic) |
| **Referral rewards** | Subscribers earn perks for referring friends | High (needs prizes) |

21. **The most effective growth is good content.** Readers forward great writing on their own. Strategy just accelerates it.

---

## 6. Metrics

| Metric | Good benchmark | How to improve |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Open rate** | > 40% (small newsletters) | Better subject lines, consistent send time |
| **Click rate** | > 5% | Better CTAs, more valuable links |
| **Subscriber growth** | > 5% / month (early) | Promotion strategy, lead magnets |
| **Unsubscribe rate** | < 1% / issue | Content quality, don't over-promote |

22. **Open rate is the most important metric.** Low open rate = unattractive subject lines or wrong send frequency.
23. **Unsubscribes aren't a bad thing.** People who don't want it leaving = the people who stay are real readers.

---

## 7. Common Traps

| Trap | Counter |
|------|---------|
| "I'll start when I'm ready" | The first issue doesn't need to be perfect, just exist |
| Chasing subscriber count, ignoring engagement | 100 engaged readers > 1000 inactive |
| Inconsistent sends | A fixed cadence — even if it's monthly |
| Every issue is a long-form essay | Mix short and long |
| Using the newsletter as an SEO tool | A newsletter is a relationship tool, not a traffic funnel |
| Charging too early | Build a free list (500+) before considering paid tier |

---

## Pre-Send Checklist

Before pressing send:

- [ ] Subject ≤ 50 characters, promises specific value
- [ ] First 2 lines land the point
- [ ] Paragraphs ≤ 3 sentences, with visual breaks
- [ ] One clear CTA
- [ ] Mobile preview reads cleanly
- [ ] Send time matches reader habits
